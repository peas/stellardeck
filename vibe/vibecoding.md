# What is vibe coding?

A way of writing software where the developer describes intent
and the AI fills in the implementation.

---

# Three skills

Curiosity, taste, and judgment

---

# Curiosity

Pull on threads. Read code from libraries you depend on.
Don't accept "it just works" as an answer.

---

# Taste

Know what good looks like before you ship it.
You can't ask AI to make something tasteful if you can't recognize it.

---

# Judgment

When to ship. When to refactor. When to throw away.
This is the most expensive thing to learn, and the least teachable.

---

---

# Read the docs

Even if you think you know the API.

---

# Read the source

When the docs are wrong or missing.

---

# Read the tests

Tests are the truth. Docs lie. Source is dense.

---

#[fit] Taste

---

# Compare three options

Don't pick the first thing that works.

---

# Look at competitors

What did they choose? Why?

---

# Show your work to people

Get reactions before you commit.

---

#[fit] Judgment

---

# Ship small

Big batches die in review.

---

# Refactor with intent

Not because it's "cleaner". Because it unblocks a future change.

---

# Throw away

Code is cheap. Sunk cost is expensive.

#[fit] The new dev

Curious. Tasteful. Decisive.

---

# Curious about the machine

How does the model work? What can it do? What can't it?

---

# Tasteful about the output

Reject mediocre. Reject generic. Reject "good enough".

---

# Decisive about direction

Choose. Move. Adjust. Repeat.
